ABOUT

Cadenza was created in response to a growing trend in musical theater, where the orchestra consists of a small rock combo plus a synthesizer player who simulates an entire orchestra. These synthesizer parts usually call for hundreds of patches, with complicated layering, and often with very fast changes between them. Programming a hardware synthesizer manually was too complicated, and software solutions required high-end computers and expensive sample libraries.

A program called Qualm was created to combine the cheapness of the hardware solution with the flexibility of the software solution. Cadenza was initially just a graphical interface for Qualm, but over time morphed into its own product.

So far, Cadenza has been used for many musicals, including a production of Children of Eden where it was used to control all 3 keyboard parts as well as the mallet percussion using a MalletKAT. Send us information about your performance setups so we can continue to improve!
